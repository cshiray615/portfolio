How I work.

Most learning problems aren't learning problems. They're process problems, tool problems, or seventeen-spreadsheets-all-claiming-to-be-the-single-source-of-truth problems. The work is figuring out which — before anyone builds a course.

Diagnose first
Find the real cause.
Five "whys" before any design move. If the answer is "the process is broken," I say so — and we fix the process, not the people.
Design with evidence
Less box-check.
Every design choice cites a principle. Cognitive load, signaling, dual channels, generative activity. If it can't earn its place, it doesn't ship.
Defend the call
Stakeholders, partnered.
A design defense isn't "no." It's "here's what this choice costs the learner — and here's a better path." Stakeholders become collaborators, not opponents.

Onboarding · Branching simulation
"Day One,
For Real."
Fortune 500 retailer · 14k frontline hires/year
Storyline Branching Voice-over SCORM
ProblemA 4-hour compliance video had a 38% completion rate. New hires were skipping out — and managers were re-teaching everything in week one anyway.
SolutionCut the video to 22 minutes of essential content. Replaced the rest with a branching shift-simulation where hires made the same decisions they'd face on the floor — with consequences they could feel.
OutcomeCompletion jumped to 94%, manager re-teach time dropped ~60%, and 30-day retention improved by 11 points.

Leadership · Cohort program
Manager
Defense.
SaaS scale-up · 120 new managers
Cohort design Coaching Async + live Capstone
ProblemNew managers were promoted on technical chops, then handed a leadership LMS path and a "good luck." 65% reported feeling unprepared at the 90-day check-in.
SolutionAn 8-week cohort: short async micro-lessons + a live coaching session each Friday, capped by a capstone where managers defended one real decision they'd made in the last 30 days.
Outcome90-day "feel prepared" scores climbed from 35% → 82%, and the cohort design became the default for every leadership level above.

04

How a project runs.

Four phases, in order. Most projects skip phase 4 — that's usually why they don't stick. I won't.

01
Spot it.
Interviews + observations. Define the actual performance gap — not the stated training request.
02
Explain it.
Map the cognitive load. Name the principles that apply. Decide whether training is part of the answer at all.
03
Fix it.
Build the lightest intervention that solves the problem — course, simulation, job aid, process change, or a mix.
04
Defend it.
Measure. Adjust. Help stakeholders see why each design choice earned its place. Hand off something they can maintain.
