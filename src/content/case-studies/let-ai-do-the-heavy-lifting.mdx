<ProblemBlock>

Most legacy Storyline content lives in a folder somewhere with no inventory, no metadata, and no shared understanding of what's still accurate. Before you can redesign a course, retire a module, or even answer "what do we have on topic X," you have to crack open each `.story` file and look at every slide.

That audit work is the gating step. It eats weeks of designer time before any actual design happens — and because it's manual, it tends to be incomplete, inconsistent across reviewers, and immediately stale.

</ProblemBlock>

<ProcessBlock>

The .story format is a zipped archive of XML, audio, and assets. Extracting structured content from it doesn't require Articulate's runtime — just the right parser and some patience.

I built a small pipeline: extract slide text, narration scripts, and structural metadata from each `.story` file, then run the extracted content through an LLM for inventory tasks the audit used to do by hand — classify each slide by topic, flag dead links, detect duplicate content across modules, surface objectives that don't map to any slide.

The output is a CSV plus a one-page summary the designer reads in twenty minutes instead of two weeks. The designer still owns the judgment calls (is this still accurate? does this still belong?) — the AI just stops them having to *find* the questions first.

</ProcessBlock>

The pipeline collapsed a multi-week manual audit into hours of designer review. The team got their pre-design time back — and started using it for the part of the job that actually requires a human (designing).

Presented the approach at DevLearn 2025 to about 200 attendees. The repeat question afterward wasn't "how does the AI work?" — it was "how do I get my org to stop treating audit work as design work?"

</OutcomeBlock>

<LessonsBlock>

The pipeline is the easy part. The harder part is convincing the team that AI-summarized inventory is *trustworthy enough to act on* — which mostly came down to keeping a designer-in-the-loop review step for any decision that retired or rewrote content. Speed without that step is a liability.

</LessonsBlock>
