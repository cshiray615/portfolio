<ProblemBlock>

Tamrack, Inc. is a cleaning supply company serving hospitals and nursing homes. Founded in 2018 in Boise, scaled to 200+ employees across five states. Their Learning and Performance Solutions (LPS) team moved New Employee Onboarding (NEO) fully online during COVID, kept it online for cost reasons, and revised it several times since. Every revision was based on informal feedback. Nobody had ever run a formal evaluation.

Meanwhile HR had flagged employee turnover as a priority and wanted to know whether NEO was actually supporting role readiness and retention. The LPS Director brought us in to find out.

</ProblemBlock>

<ProcessBlock>

Goal-based, formative evaluation. Scope limited to the structured first-week experience. Pre-boarding and the informal one-month integration period were out of scope by client request.

Open-ended responses and interview transcripts were coded by theme and sentiment using a custom codebook the team developed. Each source got a rubric rating (Exceeds / Met / Improvement Needed / Serious Problems), then triangulated to a dimension-level rating using a dimensional triangulation worksheet.

<LessonsBlock>

Two things I'd carry forward. First, **survey ceiling effects are real**: several items maxed out near 5.0 with no variance, making them useless for year-over-year comparison. Next iteration I'd write items that distinguish *good* from *great*, not items that just confirm the experience was satisfactory. Second, **client-conducted interviews skew the data**. Our client (the LPS Director) ran all the interviews, which probably softened the feedback. The interview and survey findings generally agreed, which partly offsets the risk, but next time the evaluation team should conduct interviews directly, with the client introduced separately if rapport matters.

</LessonsBlock>

<Appendix title="Methodology and instruments">

The team developed four instruments mapped to the two evaluation dimensions, plus rubrics for source-level and dimension-level scoring.

- **Instruments:** Manager survey, Participant survey, Manager interview script, Participant interview script (each with consent form and invitation email)
- **Rubrics:** Source-level (4-point scale: Exceeds / Met / Improvement Needed / Serious Problems) + Dimensional Triangulation Rubric for combining source ratings into a dimension rating
- **Coding:** Qualitative codebook developed iteratively, applied to open-ended survey responses and interview transcripts (themes + sentiment)
- **Sampling:** Surveys were sent to all 2025 NEO participants and their managers. Interview participants were recruited voluntarily from survey respondents.
- **Limitations acknowledged in the final report:** small sample (n=27 + n=14), cross-sectional design with recall variability, self-report bias, client involvement in data collection, single upstream stakeholder representation.

Final deliverables (Statement of Work, Final Report, Instruments + Rubrics) are available on request. Team CARP, Boise State OPWL 530, Spring 2026.
