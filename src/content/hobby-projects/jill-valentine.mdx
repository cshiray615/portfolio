## The character

Jill Valentine from the original *Resident Evil* (1996), in her S.T.A.R.S. (Special Tactics And Rescue Service) Alpha Team uniform. Beret, vest with hard shoulder pads, gloves, sidearm. The look that anchored every cover of the franchise for a decade.

## The build

<section class="ws-steps" aria-label="Build process">
  <article class="ws-step">
    <span class="ws-step-num" aria-hidden="true">01</span>
    <div class="ws-step-content">
      <h3 class="ws-step-label">Custom shirt pattern</h3>
      <p class="ws-step-text">Drafted the S.T.A.R.S. shirt from scratch on the dress form. Off-the-shelf patterns don't get the cut right, and the silhouette is the whole tell.</p>
      <img class="ws-step-img" src="/hobbies/costuming/jill-valentine-shirt-drafting.JPG" alt="Pattern muslin pinned and drafted on the dress form" loading="lazy" />
    </div>
  </article>

  <article class="ws-step">
    <span class="ws-step-num" aria-hidden="true">02</span>
    <div class="ws-step-content">
      <h3 class="ws-step-label">Handmade shoulder pads</h3>
      <p class="ws-step-text">Built from leather and foam. The pads carry the whole silhouette of the vest. They had to hold their shape without going stiff, so each one is a leather shell over shaped foam.</p>
      <img class="ws-step-img" src="/hobbies/costuming/jill-valentine-armor-making.JPG" alt="Shoulder pad components in progress: leather, foam, and webbing on the workbench" loading="lazy" />
    </div>
  </article>

  <article class="ws-step">
    <span class="ws-step-num" aria-hidden="true">03</span>
    <div class="ws-step-content">
      <h3 class="ws-step-label">Altered retail</h3>
      <p class="ws-step-text">Boots and pants bought off the rack, then altered for fit and accuracy. Some battles aren't worth fighting from scratch.</p>
    </div>
  </article>
</section>
